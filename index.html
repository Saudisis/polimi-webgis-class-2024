Landslides in Italy

Italy's diverse landscape and climate make it highly prone to landslides, particularly in the mountainous northern regions. These areas, characterized by steep slopes and unique geological formations, are especially vulnerable. Frequent landslides in these regions have historically caused significant damage to communities and infrastructure. Contributing factors include heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, and seismic activity, which destabilize the terrain. Efforts to mitigate these risks involve advanced monitoring systems and improved land management practices, but the threat of landslides remains a persistent challenge for Italy.

Area of Study

Valdisotto, or Val de Sota in Lombard, is a picturesque comune in the Province of Sondrio, Lombardy, Italy. Located 140 kilometers northeast of Milan and 45 kilometers northeast of Sondrio, it spans 91.57 square kilometers and has a population of 3,546. Valdisotto borders Bormio, Grosio, Sondalo, Valdidentro, and Valfurva. In July 1987, Valdisotto was devastated by a landslide from Monte Coppetto during the Valtellina flood, destroying the village of Sant'Antonio Morignone and the district of Morignone. This tragic event has left a lasting impact on the area.

Project Generalities

The coordinate reference system (CRS) used is EPSG:32632 - WGS 84 / UTM zone 32N, with units measured in meters. This system employs the Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) method for mapping the Earth, based on the World Geodetic System 1984 ensemble (EPSG:6326), which offers an accuracy of up to 2 meters. The project visualization comprises three principal parts: the methodology, the results and analysis, and the WebGIS for interactive map visualization.
